Also known as "center clearing" (中抜き - nakanuki) or "mid-air clearing" (空中消し - kuuchuu keshi), this refers to placements that seem to create a cavity at first glance, but leave a clean shape by clearing lines.

Producing a Flat Stack

Skimming to produce a flat stack.
Placing the I-piece flat can surprisingly clear lines in unexpected places. Useful for handling I-pieces that would be obstructive if placed vertically, such as in advanced stages of TAP.
