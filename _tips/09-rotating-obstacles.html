Skip / Pass (飛ばし・渡し - Tobashi / Watashi)

J-piece "skip" (飛ばし - tobashi) utilizing the conspiracy in reverse.
I-piece skip, commonly called "Patapata" (パタパタ).
Can also be used in conjunction with Synchro.
A technique to cross a 2-column wide hole by rotating twice to orient the 3-wide side downwards.
Since it involves another rotation after crossing, it's called a "3-Rotation Pass" (３回転渡し - 3-kaiten watashi).
The key is whether you can perform the first two rotations quickly.
Learning the feel of intentionally delaying the DAS charge makes it easier.
Passing to the left requires less space compared to the right.
It's fine if the piece drops on the first horizontal move or when crossing, but if there's no step down, the timing becomes extremely tight.
One method is the "Feint" (フェイント) technique: at the moment of appearance, quickly input "right, left charge" and perform the second rotation during that time.
