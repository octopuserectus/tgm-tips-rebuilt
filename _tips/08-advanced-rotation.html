Rotation Placement (回転入れ - Kaiten-ire)

In BPS-style rotation, these might not rotate or might jump upwards, but the Sega-style rotation rule "rotate such that the height of the lowest edge doesn't change" makes it very easy to use.
Thanks to the wall kicks introduced in Chapter 2, it rotates with either rotation direction.
The J-piece and L-piece also fit.
Be careful here, as the rotation direction is limited depending on the orientation.
Rotation placement called "Nurikabe" (ぬりかべ).
It requires two rotations but isn't that difficult.
It's not necessary to hold left; rotating with the lever neutral is OK.
Try to use it actively from early on and overcome the fear that it might slide down.
Since the S-piece and J-piece are easy to move left, and the Z-piece and L-piece are easy to move right, these are extremely easy to use and effective.

Rotation Placement Applying Wall Kicks

Thanks to wall kicks, you can rotate quickly while holding DAS right.
Normally, you'd have to place an O-piece first and then process it, but this troublesome shape can be neatly resolved.
Be careful, as this won't rotate unless fully inserted deep inside.
Rotation placement called "Denguri-gaeshi" (でんぐり返し - somersault).
It allows passing through a 1-column wide hole and is useful for recovering from I-piece misplacements, etc.

Mihara's Conspiracy (陰謀 - Inbou)

→
In cases where "it overlaps the stack, and there's space to shift to either side", it's set up to shift to the right; this is called the conspiracy (陰謀 - inbou).
Originally, it derived from wanting to shift the J-piece left as shown, but it went right (even if holding left on the lever), leading to comments like "It's the developers' conspiracy!".
Rotation placement called "Fuse" (伏せ).
Requires some practice to distinguish the setup, but due to the aforementioned conspiracy, you can rotate while holding left on the lever.
It's fast and leaves a clean shape afterwards. Good for time attacks.
J-piece Fuse is possible if there's terrain preventing a rightward shift.

Cases Where Wall Kicks Don't Occur

・The O-piece, of course, and the I-piece do not wall kick.
・IRS means "rotate then appear", so wall kicks do not occur.
・Correction is horizontal only. You cannot kick off the ground or ceiling.
・For T, J, and L pieces, if only the center column overlaps, wall kick doesn't happen. (For example, the J-piece shown would actually get stuck and be unmovable).
