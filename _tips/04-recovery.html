Don't Stack Blocks Above Holes

Recovery (復活 - fukkatsu) means clearing all the lines of the stack that form the lid over the hole.
Therefore, making the lid as thin as possible makes recovery easier.
While it's counterproductive if avoiding the hole excessively disrupts the stack shape, simply being conscious of "not needlessly stacking above the hole" should make a considerable difference in recovery efficiency.
→ →
Standing blocks up means you can make them narrower horizontally.
Keep this concept in mind as it's helpful for avoiding stacking over holes.
→ →
As introduced in Chapter 2, Z-pieces and S-pieces are often stacked vertically, but doing so means the lid never gets cleared. Using a T-piece is OK; this is a fairly common pattern.

Using a Misdrop as a Floor (Keeping the Top Flat)

Due to an I-piece misplacement etc., a roof-like overhang in the stack can occur.
If you're too focused on clearing lines and try to insert a T-piece or S-piece vertically, you'll be left with a troublesome shape.
The key is using the J-piece flat. This keeps the top flat, allowing for a clean clear afterwards.

Develop the mindset to place the J-piece first, even before filling the left side.
